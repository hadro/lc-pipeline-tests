OFFICIAL HISTORY OF FREE
MASONRY
Among the Colored People
in North America.
By W. H. GRIMSHAW, of the Library of Congress.
ILLUSTRATED.
WITH AN INTRODUCTION BY
JOSEPH TYLER BUTTS.
This is a history of Colored Freemasonry, published in
one volume, now ready for distribution.
It begins with the Mother Grand Lodge, London, Eng-
land, 1717, and traces the growth of Masonry (especially
among Colored men) down to the present day. It gives an
account of the introduction of Freemasonry into America;
its origin and practice among Negroes, and indicates where
Colored Masons are recognized throughout the world.
The history of Freemasonry among Colored Men in each
State is briefly and officially treated, and the book will be
an official reference for all time.
Many curious incidents are mentioned—such as initiation
of whites into colored lodges and colored men into white
lodges, also the number of Masons in the United States.
