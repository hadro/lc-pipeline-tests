PARTY LINE AND EXTENSION TELEPHONE HINTS
EFFICIENT PARTY LINE SERVICE depends largely on the consideration and courtesy
which each subscriber gives to the other parties on his line. Endeavor to limit your calls
to five minutes duration.
When dialing on a party line, or any line with an extension telephone, it is necessary to
consider the possibility that someone else may be using the line. Learn and observe the
following rules and be a courteous party line neighbor:
1. If you hear a conversation when lifting
the receiver, the line is being used.
Replace the receiver and try the call
again later.
2. If you hear the sound of "Dialing
Clicks" when lifting the receiver, it
means that someone else on the line is
dialing a number. The lifting of your
receiver has interfered with the dialing
and you should inform him to dial the
number again. Hang up your re-
ceiver and permit him to dial the num-
ber again.
3. If you are dialing and hear another
party lift his receiver before you have
heard the "Ringing Signal," inform
him that the line is in use and after he
has replaced his receiver, replace your
own for a few moments. Then remove
it and dial the complete number
again.
4. When another party lifts his receiver
after you have heard the "Ringing
Signal," it will not be necessary to re-
dial the number. Merely inform him
that the line is in use.
