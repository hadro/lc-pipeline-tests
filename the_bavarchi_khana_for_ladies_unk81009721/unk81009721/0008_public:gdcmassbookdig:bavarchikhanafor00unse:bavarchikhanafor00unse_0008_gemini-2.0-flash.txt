13.

Pickles.

BEEF PICKLE.

Two lbs. of coarse salt; half an oz. of salt-
petre; quarter of an oz. of saleratus; quarter of
an oz. of cloves, ginger and allspice; mix well
together, and rub the beef well every day for
two weeks, turning it every day; after that
cover it with molasses, and it will keep for a
year.

PICKLED ONIONS.

Choose small white onions; put them in a
strong brine for four days; then drain and pour
boiling water over them; let them stand for an
hour; drain, and wipe them dry; put them in
jars, and pour over them boiling hot vinegar,
spiced with white pepper, mace, and cloves.

PICKLED CABBAGE.

Shave one head of red cabbage; sprinkle
salt over it, and let it stand all night; in the
morning drain off the water, and put it in a kettle,
with enough vinegar to cover it; add one tea-
spoonful of ground black pepper, one teaspoonful
of ground allspice, one teaspoonful of ground
cloves, and one teaspoonful of cayenne pepper;
let it scald until tender, then put it in a jar, and
cover it with vinegar. It will keep a long time.
