At the opening of our new building in
last October, a public reception was tend-
ered the friends and patrons of the Bank,
on which occasion the Secretary read a
report containing a brief history of the
organization and development of the
Bank. The following extract from that
report is here reproduced:

"The history of the organization and
development of this business enterprise
is not entirely free from some of the essen-
tial features of a romance. To have been
born into an almost abject poverty
and to have grown into a vig-
orous prosperity, happens not often in
the history either of individuals or
institutions. Banks are a commercial
necessity in all business centers. They
are the medium through which the mon-
ey property of individuals changes own-
ership. They touch and control the mon-
ey pulse of a community. As a rule,
