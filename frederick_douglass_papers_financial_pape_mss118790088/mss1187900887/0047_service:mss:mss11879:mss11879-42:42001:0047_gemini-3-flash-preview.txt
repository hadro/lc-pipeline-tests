"Our business has been conducted, as
nearly as we knew how, along the line of
a general banking business, receiving de-
posits, both as 'call' deposits and 'time'
deposits; making loans both on real es-
tate and collateral securities, and on en-
dorsed notes where the endorsements ap-
peared safe and satisfactory. In this
way, counting our deposits, checks, loans
made and bills receivable paid, the total
bulk of our business transactions since
our organization, nearly four years ago,
has amounted to nearly two millions of
dollars.
"This statement may perhaps startle
some as it startled us, but our books will
show that we have handled just that
amount of business over our counter in
receiving and paying out money. Our
capital stock in the meantime has grown
from $6,000 to $50,000, and the amount
actually paid in has grown from $1,000
to $33,000.
